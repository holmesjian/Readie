# Readie Demo Guide

This guide will help you deliver an impressive demo of Readie at the Push-to-Prod Hackathon.

## 🎬 Demo Script (5 minutes)

### Act 1: The Problem (30 seconds)

**Say:**
> "You can do great work all week and still walk into a meeting unprepared. Not because you didn't try — but because translating a week's worth of emails and updates into the right message for the right audience takes time nobody has."

**Show:** Open Readie - point to the sidebar with 4 different meeting types.

### Act 2: The Magic (2 minutes)

**Say:**
> "Readie reads your week for you. Let me show you with my real Gmail."

**Do:**
1. Click "Fetch recent" in Gmail panel
2. Show your actual emails appearing
3. Select 2-3 relevant emails (click the + button)
4. Watch brief auto-generate

**Say:**
> "That's a brief for my stakeholder review. But watch what happens when I use the SAME emails for my 1:1 with my manager..."

**Do:**
1. Click "Weekly · Manager" in the sidebar
2. Show how the brief completely changes - same data, different framing

**Key callout:**
> "Notice - the stakeholder brief says 'backend dependency gap' but my 1:1 brief mentions 'Wei' and 'sprint 3 scoping'. Same information, different audience."

### Act 3: The Download (1 minute)

**Say:**
> "And when you're ready for the meeting..."

**Do:**
1. Click Download button
2. Show the PDF opening - clean, professional, ready to print

**Say:**
> "Professional PDF, ready to go. No formatting, no copy-paste, no context switching."

### Act 4: The Power (30 seconds)

**Say:**
> "Here's what makes this powerful - it's not just summarizing. It's TRANSLATING. Same facts, four different stories:"

**Show each meeting type quickly:**
- 1:1: "Candid, complete internal details"
- Team Sync: "Collaborative, surfaces blockers"  
- Stakeholder Review: "Business language, no jargon"
- Customer Call: "Customer-first framing only"

### Closing (30 seconds)

**Say:**
> "Your week, already translated. That's Readie."

**Bonus if time:**
> "And it's a single HTML file. No installation, no build step, no backend. Download it, open it, and go."

## 🎯 Demo Tips

### Before the Demo

1. **Test your Gmail connection** in claude.ai
2. **Have 3-5 real work emails** in your inbox about a real project
3. **Practice the flow** 2-3 times (it takes ~4 minutes)
4. **Pre-open claude.ai** with Readie loaded

### During the Demo

**DO:**
- ✅ Use REAL emails (shows it actually works)
- ✅ Show the side-by-side comparison (1:1 vs Stakeholder)
- ✅ Let the AI generate live (shows it's real, not canned)
- ✅ Point out specific details that changed between briefs

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Use sample data (judges have seen enough fake demos)
- ❌ Apologize if it takes 5 seconds to generate
- ❌ Explain every feature (focus on the core magic)
- ❌ Get bogged down in technical details

### If Something Goes Wrong

**Gmail doesn't connect:**
> "Let me use file upload instead - same result." (Have an email saved as .txt backup)

**Generation is slow:**
> "While this generates, notice the interface - clean, purposeful, no clutter."

**PDF download fails:**
> "The brief is the product - the PDF is just convenience. You can see everything you need right here."

## 🎨 Visual Highlights to Point Out

1. **The Color-Coded Agenda Items**
   - Green circle = Wins
   - Amber = Risks/Decisions
   - Red = Alerts
   - Gray = AOB

2. **The Source Tags**
   - Every bullet shows where it came from: [EMAIL] [TEAMS] [SLACK]
   - Shows traceability - "this isn't made up"

3. **The Expect Questions**
   - Indented with => arrows
   - Shows Readie anticipates pushback
   - "It's not just summarizing - it's preparing you for battle"

4. **The Option A/B Format**
   - Decision items show trade-offs clearly
   - Professional presentation of choices

## 📊 Judge Questions & Answers

**Q: "How does this compare to just using ChatGPT?"**
> "ChatGPT gives you one output. Readie gives you four - each tuned for a different audience. It's not about what happened, it's about how to frame it. And it knows meeting types - 1:1s need candor, stakeholders need business language."

**Q: "What's your business model?"**
> "API costs are tiny - $0.003 per brief. We could charge $10/month and be profitable at 100 users. But the real play is enterprise - integration with Slack, Teams, Calendar. This is an MVP that proves the concept."

**Q: "Why single file? Isn't that limiting?"**
> "It's a feature, not a bug. Zero friction adoption. No IT approval, no installation, no security review. Email it, Slack it, bookmark it. It just works."

**Q: "Can it handle large volumes of emails?"**
> "Current version processes ~10-15 emails efficiently. For scale, we'd add filtering and prioritization. But most meeting prep doesn't need 100 emails - it needs the RIGHT 5-10."

**Q: "What about privacy/security?"**
> "Nothing is stored. API calls are ephemeral. The HTML runs client-side. For enterprise, we'd add self-hosted API options and compliance certifications."
